Finding Harmony: Screen Time & Real Life

What is Digital Wellbeing?

Digital wellbeing isn't about giving up your phone or quitting gaming forever. It’s about feeling in control of the technology you use, instead of letting your technology control you. It means using devices intentionally so they don't harm your mental health, sleep, or friendships.

Healthy Screen Habits
  • • Setting a custom limit on scrolling apps.
  • • Leaving your phone outside the room during bed time.
  • • Turning off non-essential notifications while studying.
Unhealthy Habits
  • • "Doomscrolling" late into the night before an exam.
  • • Feeling anxious or angry when you aren't online.
  • • Ignoring friends or family to play one more match.

Social Media & Mental Health

Social media is awesome for keeping in touch with friends and discovering creative trends. However, it’s designed to keep you hooked, which can sometimes make us feel stressed, left out, or under pressure to look perfect.

The Bright Side

Finding your community, expressing yourself artistically, and supporting global causes instantly.

The Pressure Side

Comparing your real life to someone else's edited highlight reel, leading to FOMO (Fear of Missing Out).

Mindful Check-In Trick

Before you open an app, pause for 5 seconds and ask yourself: "Why am I opening this? Am I bored, lonely, or truly looking for something?" This breaks the auto-pilot loop!
